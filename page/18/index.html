If you’re just shy of Central Park to the south and it’s in the middle of the day, this might be a good option. This is a local restaurant to Long Island City that classifies itself as an Italian Argentine restaurant. The place is super small, but it feels nice and cozy. This is another restaurant on my list to go. I am not crazy about Italian, but I was hungry and wanted something pretty straight forward. This little pizza shop is down right at the edge of South/West Village close to a Shake Shack. The panzerottis are like super thin calzones that are frieds as oppose to baked. The dough that they use here is really tasty. It’s chewy, has a nice bounce, and has a very nice flavor profile to it. The dough definitely has a distinct flavor to it that makes it taste super fresh.
