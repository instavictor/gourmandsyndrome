Posted September 25, 2021

This is located right next to the water near restaurants like Maiella and American Brass. Whenever we try walk-in, they always tells us it’s an hour wait even when there’s no one outside. Since we didn’t have anything else close by that we wanted to try, we were able to book a reservation for an early Saturday dinner again.

Ambiance

For some reason this restaurant is very popular with a large latin clientele. People will have massive birthday parties or get togethers.

The interior of the restaurant is no frills and reminds me a lot of Florida latin restaurants. They have an outdoor patio set both in front of the restaurant and in the back. When we went in, there was a host in front of the restaurant and only let certain parties go in. When they let us go in, we had to wait a few minutes for a hostess inside to seat us.

The food came out pretty fast, but the service was kind of a hit and miss. Towards the end of our dinner, I had to flag the waitress for our bill.

Be aware that when paying by card, the hostess brings a card reader with 20% pre-selected. She told us to just “sign”, but I changed the tip to what I wanted to give.

Food

The pina colada (left) came with coconut cream, pineapple juice, and coconut rum.

The spicy orchard (right) came with jalape√±o infused tequila, green apples, lime, agave, and a spiced rim. My partner thought this was really spicy, but I felt the heat level was fairly grounded. The apples pretty much soaked all the tequila, so be a little cautious when biting those. Other than that, it was pretty standard.

The churrasco came with center cut prime skirt steak, white rice, beans, maduros, chimichurri, and red onion escabeche. The steak was ordered medium rare and was fairly tender. The flavor profile of this dish was pretty much what you would expect when you order skirt steak done latin style.

The pernil came with cuban marinated pulled pork, rice, beans, maduros, and lime and garlic mojo. This actually came on a pretty large bone. Some of the tendons were crispy or soft, so they were edible. The tender pork itself tasted very standard for cuban style pork.

Final Verdict

The food was pretty good but also fairly typical. The service is kind of average, but if you’re looking for a pretty no frills latin restaurant, you can’t really go wrong with this.
