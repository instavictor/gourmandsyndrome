Spaghetti Incident

NYC
Manhattan
LES
Italian
Yelp
Google
Posted September 19, 2019

Summary

This is another restaurant on my list to go. I am not crazy about Italian, but I was hungry and wanted something pretty straight forward.

The lasagna bolognese came with ground beef, béchamel, and tomato sauce. The pasta consisted of several very thin sheets with a thin layer of ground beef between each. Careful though, the lasagna was piping hot. It was pretty good, but I think I would’ve preferred more sauce and more cheese as an ode to the normal lasagnas you’d normally get. Otherwise, I think it was pretty good.

The chitarra came with homemade pasta, homemade mozzarella, basil, and fresh chopped tomato sauce. The pasta was al dente and deliciously chewy. I love the mozzarella and the tomatoes in this sauce. It was super fresh and clean.

Not pictured, we also god a side of olive taggiasche which is marinated imported olives from Liguria Italy. They were pitted, but still pretty good.

Service was pretty prompt, and the location is pretty quaint. Portion size was pretty average, as you won’t feel too full or too hungry after. The price wasn’t exorbant, though be forwarned that is is CASH only. I like how the theme of this food was pretty straightforward. I do wish that some of the pastas had more meats, but it is what it is.

Final Verdict

If you’re looking for pasta with not many meat options, it’s a pretty good pasta centric shop in the Lower East Side.
