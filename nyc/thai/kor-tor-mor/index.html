Summary

What's nice about Thai food is they are rarely bad, and they are very consistent even at different restaurants. We were in Cobble Hill after an errand, and this place was relatively close by and open for a late afternoon dinner.

We got 2 different Thai teas. The first was the regular Thai tea and the other is Lychee Thai tea. Both were pretty good. The lychee might be sweet for some, but it tastes literally like the fruit.

This was the pad see ew, which was basically flat rice noodles with egg, Chinese broccoli, and sauteed in sweet soy sauce. What was nice about this was the noodles weren't soggy or soft at all and actually had a nice texture to it.

This was the tom yum noodle soup with chicken, bean sprouts, scallions, cilantro, and grounded peanuts. This was actually pretty good. The noodles were similar to vermicelli. You can get this without mushrooms if desired. The broth itself was light but flavorful with a lot of that lemongrass flavor. It wasn't too spicy nor was it too mild.

For dessert, we wanted to order the mango sticky rice, but it's not in season unfortunately.

Service was fast and quick. Price was pretty good.

Final Verdict

In the Cobble Hill area and if you are craving Thai, this is a good place to check out.
