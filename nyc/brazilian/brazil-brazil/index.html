Posted December 8, 2019

Summary

We actually have eaten here before, but we totally forgot to take pictures and document. From the best recollection, we remembered this place as being one of our favorite Brazilian restaurants in NYC. So we decided to visit this again to document.

The maracuja caipirinha we recalled being really good our first time doing. Ordered it again, and it was nothing but a disappointment. The tartness of the passion fruit tasted sour and borderlinen rotten. There was no sweetness, and it tasted very watered down and unflavored. We asked the waitress to bring it back to the bartender because of this. The bartender created another one, and it was slightly better but still didn’t taste fresh.

The yucca and sausage was the appetizer part of a 3 course prix fixe. The yucca was pretty dry and so was the sausage. There wasn’t anything memorable about this although it reminded me of street food.

The picanha na chapa was ordered medium rare, but eventually became well done. The meat wasn’t as tender as other Brazilian places.

The side of couve was actually pretty decent. Usually other places don’t seem to salt/season this, but this place actually seasoned it.

The feijoada came with black bean pork stew, dry beef and sausage, with collared greens, orange, farofa, and rice. This black bean stew was actually pretty good and pretty flavorful.

The maracuja mousse was alright. The flavors were all there, but there wasn’t anything incredible about it.

Final Verdict

The food felt like it wasn’t that good. I am not sure if memories were making us jaded, but there are probably better Brazilian places to go to around this area.
